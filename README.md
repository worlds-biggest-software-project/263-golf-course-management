# Golf Course Management

> Part of the [worlds-biggest-software-project](https://github.com/worlds-biggest-software-project) initiative.
>
> An open, AI-native platform for tee time booking, membership management, POS, and course conditions across public, private, and resort golf operations.

Golf Course Management is a candidate project to build an integrated management system for golf courses and clubs. It targets operators who today must choose between marketplace-dependent SaaS, legacy private-club suites, or single-purpose tournament tools — with no open alternative that unifies the tee sheet, pro shop, F&B, membership, and handicap reporting under operator control.

---

## Why Golf Course Management?

- **Marketplace dependency**: GolfNow's revenue-share model couples course tee-time distribution to a single consumer marketplace, limiting data control for operators.
- **Ecosystem lock-in**: Chronogolf is tied to the Lightspeed commerce stack, narrowing hardware and integration choices.
- **Legacy architecture in private clubs**: Jonas Club Management offers deep membership billing but with complex setup and dated foundations.
- **Fragmented tooling**: Golf Genius leads tournament management but requires a separate PMS alongside it; Club Prophet has long tenure but a dated interface.
- **Pricing pressure**: Operational PMS platforms typically run USD 300–800 per month, with private-club suites reaching tens of thousands annually — leaving smaller courses underserved.

## AI-Native Advantage

AI is applied to operational decisions that incumbent tools surface only as static reports. Demand-based tee-time pricing reacts in real time to weather, competitor availability, seasonal patterns, and booking pace. Member-retention models flag at-risk members from declining visits or spend and trigger personalised offers before they lapse. Course conditions reporting fuses turf sensors, weather APIs, and staff notes into guest-facing updates, while predictive maintenance scheduling anticipates turf and irrigation work from usage and weather-stress patterns. Tournament pairing and scheduling optimisation balances skill levels and reduces pace-of-play disruption.

## Tech Stack & Deployment

The project is intended to align with established golf and payments standards: GHIN and the World Handicap System for handicap calculation and submission, EMV and contactless requirements at POS terminals, and PCI DSS for all card processing across pro shop, F&B, and online booking. Tee time distribution should follow emerging Open Tee Time API conventions used by third-party booking channels. Specific deployment modes and SDK choices are open and will be defined during specification.

## Market Context

The golf course management software market is projected at approximately USD 550 million in 2026, growing to USD 885 million by 2034 at a CAGR of 8.4%; the broader global golf software market (including player apps and handicap systems) was valued at USD 2.4 billion in 2023. Incumbent operational PMS platforms charge USD 300–800 per month, full private-club suites command enterprise pricing in the tens of thousands annually, and tournament-only tools start around USD 700 per year. Primary buyers are public green-fee operators, private and semi-private club managers, resort golf directors, and charity and corporate event organisers.
